Chasing Goals, Overcoming Struggles

      John Stephen K. Fajardo was born on November 30, 2004, as the eldest of three siblings.1 He grew up in Santa Maria, Bulacan, in a simple yet loving family.2 Without a room of his own, he shared a bed with his family, which strengthened their bond.3 He attended Cebu Sacred Heart College for his elementary years, where he consistently performed as an honor student.4 Many people saw him as a bright child because of his academic achievements.5 However, he was a shy kid around strangers but quite playful at home.6 His family often found him to be a bit of a nuisance, always joking around and making noise.7 Despite his playful nature, he was well-mannered and disciplined, thanks to the values instilled in him by his parents.8 One of his favorite childhood activities was playing outdoors with friends.9 He spent hours running around, climbing trees, and engaging in various games.10 Like many children, he had a few childhood crushes that made his early years more exciting.11 However, what truly captured his heart was basketball.12 He developed a deep love for the sport at an early age and played whenever he had the chance.13 Whether practicing alone or playing with friends, he always felt happiest with a basketball in his hands.14 His family supported his passion while ensuring that he remained focused on his studies.15 He admired his parents for their hard work and sacrifices to provide for the family.16 Their dedication inspired him to excel in school and make them proud.17 He also enjoyed family gatherings, where they bonded over simple meals and laughter.18 Despite the challenges of life, his childhood was filled with warmth, happiness, and valuable lessons.19 Looking back, he remains grateful for the memories, experiences, and love that shaped his early years.20

      His teenage years were a period of transformation, challenges, and unforgettable experiences.1 At the age of 13, he underwent a significant milestone when he got circumcised, marking his transition into adolescence.2 Puberty came gradually, but by the 9th grade, he experienced a sudden growth spurt that made him almost unrecognizable.3 Before entering high school, he had to leave Cebu Sacred Heart College due to financial reasons.4 As a result, he attended three different high schools, which proved to be a difficult adjustment.5 In 9th grade, he transferred to Mater Dei Academy, where he met new friends and reconnected with old classmates from elementary school.6 Some of the people he met there became lifelong friends whom he now considers family.7 During these years, he experienced his first love, which was both exciting and overwhelming.8 However, his teenage years also brought his first major heartbreak, which was difficult to overcome.9 Despite this, he found comfort in his high school friends, who were always there to support him.10 They would often go out after class, creating lasting memories together.11 Basketball remained an essential part of his life, and he continued to play regularly, even participating in mini-leagues.12 However, everything changed when the pandemic struck, forcing him to stay home and isolate from his friends.13 During lockdown, he spent most of his days watching TV, playing video games, or endlessly scrolling through his phone.14 It was a difficult period, but he eventually adapted to the situation.15 After the pandemic, he was able to reunite with his friends and make up for the lost time.16 As high school came to an end, he and his peers faced the stress of college admissions.17 They spent countless hours discussing their future and contemplating which universities to apply to.18 Despite the struggles, he cherished every moment of his teenage years.19 Looking back, those years were a mixture of growth, heartache, and happiness.20

      Entering college in 2023 was a major transition that made him realize he was no longer a high school student.1 In senior high, he took the HUMSS strand and was initially unsure about which program to pursue in college.2 After careful consideration, he decided to take a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology at the University of the East (UE)-Manila.3 Choosing a college was a challenging decision, but having multiple family members as UE alumni greatly influenced his choice.4 He understood that this was the start of real life and that college would serve as the foundation for his future.5 Adjusting to this new phase was not easy, as he deeply missed his high school friends and the familiar environment he once had.6 However, it also opened up opportunities to meet new people who shared similar interests.7 One of his biggest struggles was the daily two-hour commute from Bulacan to Manila, which was both exhausting and time-consuming.8 Academically, he struggled with computer programming at first but gradually improved and became more proficient.9 When his second year started, he found it even harder to manage his time due to longer school hours.10 He had to sacrifice a lot of sleep just to keep up with his academic workload.11 As semesters passed, he began losing touch with some friends due to different circumstances.12 He also barely had the chance to see his hometown friends and high school family, making him feel increasingly disconnected.13 Basketball, which was once a big part of his life, became something he could only enjoy during semestral breaks.14 Despite these challenges, he gained his first work experience before the second semester of his second year as an assistant or encoder.15 This opportunity gave him valuable insights into the professional world and helped him develop new skills.16 While college life has been tough, it has also taught him resilience and independence.17 He is determined to finish his studies on time to help relieve his parents of financial burdens.18 Though the road ahead is uncertain, he remains motivated to push forward and make the most out of his college years.19 Every challenge he faces now is just a stepping stone toward the future he is working hard to build.20
